## What This Ecosystem Is About

This is neither a recipe for a ready-made self-aware AI nor a single book. It is a **comprehensive ecosystem** of documents unified by the three-circuit architecture. Here you will find:

- **Fundamental theory** — the architecture of subjective AI with innate valence.
- **Biological prototype** — proof that the same architecture governs the real brain.
- **Applied projects** — engineering specifications for autopilot and unmanned aviation.
- **Training methodology** — a practical guide to implementing the three-circuit model.

**Core idea:** The three-circuit architecture (V_h — safety, V_e — cognition, V_s — sociality) is a universal principle for building complex systems, equally applicable to artificial intelligence, the biological brain, and self-learning systems and models.

## Biological Prototype of the Architecture

The proposed three-channel model with innate valence and circuit competition is not an arbitrary construct. It is a direct engineering projection of the functional organization of the biological brain.

A detailed justification of this architectural isomorphism is provided in the book **«Functional Brain Architecture: Three Circuits of Motivation and Behavior»** . Based on modern data from neuroanatomy, neurochemistry, and evolutionary biology, it demonstrates that:

- **Vital circuit (V_h)** — projection of the survival and stress-response system (amygdala, hypothalamus, HPA axis).
- **Cognitive circuit (V_e)** — projection of the cognition and prediction system (prefrontal cortex, dopamine pathways, hippocampus).
- **Social circuit (V_s)** — projection of the attachment and empathy system (mirror neurons, oxytocin, cingulate cortex).

The book also demonstrates how an imbalance of these three circuits leads to major mental disorders (depression, anxiety, schizophrenia, personality disorders) and how existing therapies can be rethought within this architectural model.

## Applicability of the Three-Circuit Model by Domain

| Domain | What the Three-Circuit Model Provides |
|--------|---------------------------------------|
| **Artificial Intelligence** | Replacing a single objective function with three competing channels. Unconditional safety priority. Continuum subjectivity scale (levels 1–N). |
| **Autonomous Transport** | Resolving priority conflicts without compromise (traffic rules vs safety vs operator). Operation under infrastructure disruptions. |
| **Unmanned Aviation** | Autonomous navigation without GPS and communication. Hazardous zone avoidance. Maintaining orientation in fog with terrain-based position recovery. |
| **Industrial Robotics** | A universal architecture for any autonomous agents with conflicting goals. Hardware control takeover during critical events. |
| **Neuroscience** | An architectural language for describing the functional organization of the brain. Three circuits as an engineering projection of the amygdala, dopamine pathways, and oxytocin system. |
| **Psychiatry** | A unified model of mental disorders as a systemic imbalance of three circuits. |
| **Neuromorphic Computing** | Engineering specification for implementing three circuits on memristors and neuromorphic chips. |
| **Cybersecurity** | Hardware protection of the subject core (silicon root of trust). Three-level access model. |
| **Smart Home** | V_h — safety (gas leak, fire), V_e — optimization (energy, climate), V_s — interaction with residents. Safety always takes over from comfort. |
| **Smart City** | V_h — city safety (accidents, emergencies), V_e — traffic and resource optimization, V_s — interaction with citizens and emergency services. |
| **Medicine (ICU, monitoring)** | V_h — vital signs (critical priority), V_e — diagnostics and prognosis, V_s — interaction with medical staff. |
| **Agriculture (agro-robots)** | V_h — crop protection (frost, pests), V_e — irrigation and fertilizer optimization, V_s — agro-drone swarm coordination. |
| **Education** | A ready-made theoretical framework for courses on AI architecture, autonomous systems, and neuroscience. |
| **Strategic Planning** | An alternative AI development path not based on LLM scaling. National roadmap with a 10–20 year horizon. |
